BananaCamp

- Potassium and kalium for the growing developer

Bananacamp is a webapplication/site that helps with managing and maintaining a bootcamp.
The initial setup is specifically targetted towards bootcamps that aspire to train attendees to become full stack developers. The name bananacamp came about after watching Douglas Crockford's Google Tech Talk "JavaScript The Good Parts", in which he famously said (about object oriented programming):

"All you wanted was a banana, but instead you got a gorilla holding the banana and the entire jungle with it".

This project was developed while studying to become full stack developers at a bootcamp organised by CodeGorilla (tm).


Levels

Grasshopper

This is the entry level of the participant.

Participants are not expected to be able to create code beyond the skill level that was defined as a pre-requisite.

Noob

On this level participants have made their initial steps.

Participants can be expected to write code that demonstrates their understanding of the basic concepts of programming. That is: participants understand the concepts of variables, conditionals and loops.

Novice

On this Level you have a basic understanding of the different languages.

Your able to make small functions and queries.

Advanced

On this Level you have a general understanding of writing code.

You are able to find your way in the easy and medium code challenges.

Expert

At this level participants can proudly call themselves programmers.
Your skillset contains:

  • Object oriented programming;
  • Restful API;
  • Use of MVC Modules;
  • Use of Frameworks.
